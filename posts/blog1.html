Why am I unemployed after doing Computer Science and Engineering?

Published in 2024

I wanted to work in the tech field because I genuinely liked using my computer. After finishing school, I chose Computer Science and Engineering and got admitted to a private college. The main subjects in computer science—Operating Systems, DBMS, Networking, and DSA—are supposed to be the foundation for anyone who wants to work in tech. But during my college years, classes were mostly online because of COVID. I didn’t really learn much. I managed to get good marks by memorizing things for exams, and then I forgot them soon after.

If someone truly wants to enter the tech field, those core subjects are mandatory. But since I was only memorizing answers to pass exams, I never properly understood them. On top of that, I struggle with a mental disorder, which makes it hard for me to retain information. I can study something and understand it a little, but after some time, I forget. Because of this, I lacked real skills, even though I had a degree.

After graduating in 2023, I enrolled in an online full-stack development course. They claimed they would teach how code is written in the industry, but the classes were very basic. I ended up teaching myself the MERN stack. Even now, I can build a basic CRUD application with authentication. But despite that, I am still not considered hireable.

During my BTech years, our teachers used to give us large sets of questions—sometimes 150 or 200 for a single subject—and tell us that the exam questions would come only from that list. So naturally, like any normal student, I memorized those answers. I wasn’t learning concepts; I was just memorizing to pass. By the time the exams were over, I was left with almost no practical knowledge. I hate this method of learning, because in the end you have a degree but no real skills.

"You can pass exams by memorizing answers. But you can’t build a career without understanding."

My father now tells me to prepare for government exams, but that also depends heavily on rote learning, which I don’t enjoy at all. Right now, I am unemployed, with no money in my pocket. This is my story, and honestly, this is what happens in many private colleges.
