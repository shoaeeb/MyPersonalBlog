Ready for Something Real

Published in 2024

Lately I’ve been feeling something that isn’t exactly depression, but it isn’t nothing either. It’s a quiet kind of sadness that sits in the background of my days. I don’t go out much. I stay at home most of the time because I don’t really have a reason to step outside. No college, no job, nowhere I’m expected to be. My world has become very small.

What’s strange is that whenever I do go out, I actually feel better. Walking outside, breathing different air, watching people pass by—it reminds me that life is still happening. But recently something inside me has changed. When I walk and see couples or notice girls my age, I feel this deep pull that I didn’t feel before. It’s not just attraction. It’s the realization that I’ve grown to a point where I genuinely want a partner in my life.

When I was younger, I didn’t think about love much. It wasn’t important. I could stay in my own world and feel fine. But now the need feels real and intense. I want companionship. I want to be close to someone, to share moments, to feel chosen and understood. It’s not about pleasure. It’s about connection.

What makes it harder is knowing that I don’t have the money or stability that I believe a relationship requires. I don’t feel “ready” in the way society tells me I should be ready. Because of that, I carry this quiet sadness. Sometimes I catch myself thinking that if I just had enough money, enough security, then maybe I could allow myself to want love. Maybe I could experience what others seem to have so naturally.

"I don’t think I am broken. I think I am human."

I don’t think I am broken. I think I am human. I think this is what happens when you grow older, when loneliness becomes more visible, when your heart starts asking for something more than survival. I don’t want luxury. I don’t want perfection. I just want a little love in my life. Maybe this sadness isn’t a sign that something is wrong with me. Maybe it’s simply a sign that I am ready for something real.
