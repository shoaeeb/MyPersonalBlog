Staying Honest With What I Want

Published in 2024

Today I found myself thinking about a decision I made last year and wondering if it was a mistake.

I had gone for an interview at a BPO company, and I actually got selected. It was a day-shift role, which is not very common in India. Still, I chose not to join. The company had very poor reviews, and something about it didn’t feel right to me. I don’t want to work in a place where I don’t feel respected.

I also know what I don’t want. I don’t like sales, and I don’t want a job that constantly pushes me to convince or chase people. What I want is a desk-based role where I can use my basic technical knowledge about computers in a calm and focused environment.

"I don’t want to work in a place where I don’t feel respected."

I’m aware that, with my specific situation, finding such a job is not easy. But for now, I’m choosing to stay honest with myself about what I want and what I don’t. Let’s see where this path takes me.
