When Silence Hurts More Than Rejection

Published in 2024

I’ve been trying to understand why something small can suddenly make my mood drop. I’m usually in a decent headspace, but sometimes one interaction is enough to pull me back into that familiar sadness.

A week ago, I created an account on a website called Chatlib, where you can talk to strangers. I’m not always active, but I message people occasionally. Recently, I started talking to someone who asked for my Instagram ID. I shared it. After that, the conversation changed. Almost immediately, she seemed uninterested in talking to me.

I asked her why.

No response.

I asked again—still nothing.

That silence bothered me more than the rejection itself.

This might be one of the fastest rejections I’ve experienced, especially from someone who was a complete stranger just minutes before. I’ve been rejected before—in interviews, in friendships, in different parts of life. I can accept rejection. It’s part of growing up. What I struggle with is not knowing why.

"At least tell me the reason."

When someone disappears without explanation, your mind starts filling in the gaps. You start questioning yourself: Did I say something wrong? Was I not interesting enough? Was it my profile? My words? Me? The lack of clarity hurts more than a simple, honest answer ever could.

I’m not angry at that person. People have the right to stop talking. But I wish we were a little more human with each other—especially online. A single sentence can bring closure. Silence only leaves confusion.

Maybe this is just how the internet works. Maybe not everyone owes us an explanation. But I believe honesty, even when it’s uncomfortable, is kinder than disappearing. And sometimes, all we really want is not acceptance—but understanding.
