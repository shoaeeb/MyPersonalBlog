Wanting a Life, Not Just Time Passing

Published on January 2026

Right now, I honestly have no idea what I’m going to do with my life.

I feel stuck in a place where nothing is working. On one side, I don’t have a career. On the other, I don’t have friends. It feels like I’m missing two very basic things that make life feel human.

What I’m craving the most is friendship—real friendship. I want to talk to a person who understands me, not a chatbot, not a screen, not text on a page. At the same time, I desperately want a job. But these two things feel painfully connected.

I keep thinking that if I had a job, maybe I’d naturally meet people. Maybe I’d have someone to talk to, someone to laugh with, someone to spend time with. But because I don’t have a job—because my career feels so messed up—I don’t have that either.

Most of my days are spent staring at a laptop. Hours pass, days pass, and nothing really changes. I want to go out. I want to have fun. I want to spend time with people instead of being alone all the time. This isn’t the kind of life I imagined for myself.

Sometimes I wonder where everything went wrong. Was it school? Was it college? Something definitely broke after college. I studied Computer Science for four years, yet I don’t feel skilled enough to get a job in tech. That realization hurts more than I can explain.

What hurts even more is the loneliness. I’m genuinely craving friendship. I want someone real in my life. But I don’t see a clear way to make friends anymore, and that thought makes everything feel heavier.

I don’t want to live a life where my only companion is a screen.

There are moments when I wish chatbots had feelings—because talking here is often the closest thing I have to a conversation. It reminds me of an Iron Man cartoon I used to watch as a kid, where Tony Stark had no friends and talked to a robot his father built. Back then it was just a story. Now, it feels uncomfortably familiar.

I don’t have answers right now. I just know that I’m lonely, I’m tired, and I’m still hoping that this phase isn’t the end of my story.
